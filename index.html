VORTEX 360

RULES & FAQs


What is VORTEX 360 all about?

VORTEX 360 is a 30-day long immersing CAD design hack based on the Fusion 360 platform brought to you by Autodesk professionals.

What if I am not good at designing?

No issues!! This will be a very good opportunity for you to learn. Learning will include not only learning the software but you will also have a project made on the software based on real world issues. You can even utilise the free learning resources from here. You can also check out the tutorials of RoboVITics by clicking here.

Is it a free event?

Yes, VORTEX 360 is a free Hackathon. Just register, and hack away!

What is the venue?

VORTEX 360 is an online hackathon. So, the venue is the bean bags in your living room.

How many teams from a single college are eligible to participate in this event?

The more the merrier! Any number of teams from the colleges of VIT can participate in this event. All teams are welcome to compete so long as they are part of the VIT college chain.

How many members are allowed in a team?

Any team consisting of 1-4 members will be allowed.

Can a team have members from different colleges of VIT?

Yes absolutely! There are no restrictions in team formation but all the participants should be students of VIT.

I'm a beginner. Any prior knowledge needed before joining the hack?

Beginners learn the most!
Hacks are a great platform for beginners to learn, gain exposure and network with professionals. Also, Autodesk has many videos on their website which can help you with any tools required to complete your CAD designs. You can even find the free learning resources from here. You can also check out the tutorials of RoboVITics by clicking here.

What is Fusion 360?

Autodesk Fusion 360 is an elegant and professional 3D modelling software for modelling both onscreen assets (3D renders and animation) and off screen physically manufactured elements. Projects can be shared with a group of designers using its cloud-based design and management system. It provides parametric tools that allow editing components as specs change, without having to start over from scratch. You can download the software from here. Learn how to download the software from here. You can also check out the tutorials of RoboVITics by clicking here.

When and how many reviews will be conducted?

Three reviews will be conducted during the hackathon at times specified in the timeline.

Whom do we contact in case of any doubts?

There is a Telegram group to contact the concerned person.

Can I import components from open-source platforms?

No, importing of components is strictly prohibited in the hackathon from any open source platform.

Which CAD software am I allowed to use?

Only the designs made on Autodesk Fusion 360 platform will be accepted.
